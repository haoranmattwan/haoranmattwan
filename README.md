### About me

My name is Haoran (He/Him), and I go by Matt.  I am a third-year graduate student in Drs. Len Green and Joel Myerson's Behavioral Economics Lab at Washington University in St. Louis.  I graduated from Reed College (Portland, Oregon) with a B.A in Psychology/Economics, where I worked in Dr. Timothy Hackenberg's Learning & Adaptive Behavior lab.  My primary research interests are behavioral economics and age-related changes in complex decision making, and I study delay discounting, probability discounting, and computational modeling of complex decision making.  

I've and will (at least try to) upload the analytic codes (mainly R) with the data for all my published research, and you may find some of them in my OSF as well.
